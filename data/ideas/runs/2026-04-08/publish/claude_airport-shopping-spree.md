# Airport Shopping Spree

**Idea ID:** `airport-shopping-spree`
**Source:** anthropic / claude-3-haiku-20240307
**Frequency:** daily

## Thesis
Travelers tend to spend more money on impulse purchases at airports compared to their typical shopping habits. By tracking airport retail sales data, we can identify patterns and profit from this behavioral trend.

## Universe
- TICKER1
- TICKER2
- TICKER3

## Data Sources
- Airport sales data from the Transportation Security Administration (TSA)
- Passenger traffic data from the Federal Aviation Administration (FAA)

## Signal Logic
When airport retail sales growth exceeds passenger traffic growth by a certain threshold (e.g., 5%), it signals an increase in impulse purchases.

## Entry / Exit
Enter long positions in airport retail stocks when the signal is triggered. Exit positions when the signal reverses.

## Position Sizing
Size positions proportionally to the strength of the signal, with larger positions taken when the spread between retail sales and passenger growth is wider.

## Risks
Potential for the signal to be influenced by external factors like economic conditions or seasonal trends. Liquidity risk for smaller airport retail stocks.

## Implementation Notes
1. Collect daily airport retail sales and passenger traffic data from the TSA and FAA. 2. Calculate the spread between the growth rates. 3. Generate buy/sell signals based on the predetermined threshold. 4. Execute trades in the corresponding airport retail stocks.
