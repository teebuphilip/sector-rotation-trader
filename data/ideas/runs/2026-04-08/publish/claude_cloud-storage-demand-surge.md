# Cloud Storage Demand Surge

**Idea ID:** `cloud-storage-demand-surge`
**Source:** anthropic / claude-3-haiku-20240307
**Frequency:** daily

## Thesis
The global shift to remote work and online learning has led to a surge in demand for cloud storage services. By monitoring web traffic and search trends, we can identify spikes in this demand and capitalize on the corresponding stock price movements of leading cloud storage providers.

## Universe
- BOX
- DROPBOX
- GOOGL
- MSFT

## Data Sources
- Google Trends
- Alexa Web Traffic Rankings
- SEC filings of major cloud storage companies

## Signal Logic
Monitor Google Trends for search terms related to cloud storage (e.g., 'cloud storage', 'online storage', 'file hosting'). Identify spikes in search interest that exceed the 90th percentile of the past 12 months. Confirm the trend with Alexa web traffic rankings for top cloud storage providers. Look for correlated increases in website traffic and user signups reported in company filings.

## Entry / Exit
Enter long positions in leading cloud storage providers when search and web traffic signals indicate a demand surge. Exit positions when the signals show a sustained decline in demand.

## Position Sizing
Size positions based on the strength of the demand signal. Allocate a larger percentage of the portfolio to positions when the demand surge is more pronounced.

## Risks
The demand surge may be temporary, and stock prices may not reflect the underlying trend. Additionally, geopolitical events or changes in technology could disrupt the cloud storage industry.

## Implementation Notes
1. Collect daily Google Trends data for relevant cloud storage search terms. 2. Monitor Alexa web traffic rankings for top cloud storage providers. 3. Analyze SEC filings for user growth and revenue trends. 4. Develop a scoring system to identify demand surges and trigger entry/exit signals.
