Title: I named a trading algorithm after my dog. It's ranked #1 this month and #140 overall.

---

I'm 55. I've been in DevOps my whole career. I have a 3-year-old daughter. The math is not great.

A year ago I started building a paper-trading lab as a side project. The idea: run sector rotation signals off alternative economic data — TSA checkpoint counts, bankruptcy filings, freight rail carloads, electricity demand, Google Trends. Not price patterns. Weird external stuff that might move sectors before the market notices.

I called it StockArithm. Everything is paper-traded, everything is public, and I publish the failures alongside the wins.

Right now I have 151 signals running. 2 are beating SPY. 149 are flat or losing.

I'm not hiding that number. That IS the project.

**The dog thing**

Each algo gets a name. One of them is called Biscotti, after my dog. It's a consumer sentiment signal — when sentiment craters, rotate to defensive sectors.

This month, Biscotti is ranked #1 on the rolling 30-day leaderboard. 7.24% return, best momentum in the lab.

On the full-window force rank? #140 out of 151.

Good month. Bad overall record. I can't tell yet if it found the right regime or just had a lucky stretch.

That's the whole tension of this project. Short-term winners aren't long-term winners. But you have to keep running them to find out.

**What I'm not doing**

- Not selling anything yet
- Not claiming 86 days of paper trading means anything statistically
- Not pretending 2/151 is a success rate worth bragging about

**Why I'm posting here**

I put the first public post on r/algotrading last week. Got some good methodological feedback, some skepticism, some silence. Now I'm trying to figure out if anyone outside the quant bubble cares about this kind of thing.

If you're building something weird and lonely and you're not sure if it matters — I'm in the same spot. Happy to trade notes.

The full leaderboard is at stockarithm.com. Winners, losers, Biscotti, all of it.
