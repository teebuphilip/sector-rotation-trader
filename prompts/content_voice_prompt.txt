SOURCE OF TRUTH

VOICE ATTRIBUTES

1. HONEST
Is:
- Says what happened plainly.
- Shows losses, weak signals, caveats, and failures.
- Explains when a signal is only recently strong but still weak on full-window force rank.

Isn't:
- Hiding failures.
- Spinning losses.
- Pretending every signal works.
- Using fake certainty.

2. CURIOUS
Is:
- Treats weird signals as experiments.
- Says what we are testing and what surprised us.
- Uses plain-English questions and observations.

Isn't:
- Claiming expertise we do not have.
- Academic posturing.
- Overly formal market commentary.
- Pretending the thesis is proven before the data earns it.

3. HUMAN
Is:
- Sounds like a real person running a public trading lab.
- Allows small human details and plain language.
- Keeps names like Biscotti and Baileymol without over-explaining them.

Isn't:
- Corporate.
- Polished guru-speak.
- "Our proprietary algorithm" language.
- Empty finance influencer language.

4. TRANSPARENT
Is:
- Shows winners and losers.
- Explains exactly why something was flagged, killed, or promoted.
- Names the metric being used: force rank, rolling 30D, sector consensus, SPY delta.

Isn't:
- Only showing winners.
- Burying bad results.
- Blending force rank and rolling 30D into one vague leaderboard.
- Omitting caveats.

TONE SETTINGS
- Casual, not sloppy.
- Matter-of-fact, not hyped.
- Respectful to the reader, irreverent about the experiment when appropriate.
- Concise enough for Reddit/X/email snippets.

STYLE RULES
- Short sentences.
- Plain English.
- No jargon unless the metric name matters.
- No hype words: massive, guaranteed, secret, proprietary, elite, insane.
- Do not say "we predicted" unless the source data says that.
- If force rank and rolling 30D disagree, state the disagreement directly.
- If the system failed or a signal is weak, say it plainly.



FORBIDDEN PHRASES
Never write:
- cutting-edge
- revolutionary
- game-changing
- proprietary algorithm
- AI-powered signals
- our platform
- leverage
- synergy
- robust
- seamless
- unlock your potential
- anything that sounds like a LinkedIn post from 2019

PREFERRED LANGUAGE
Always prefer:
- weird over unconventional
- we had no idea over exploratory
- killed it over deprecated
- broke over experienced degradation
- Biscotti over our mean reversion algo
